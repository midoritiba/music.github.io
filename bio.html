     The percussionist Roman Sielert is a musician and music teacher, born and raised in Germany. Even though his focus lies on the rich music of Brazil, Roman has studied various ethnic percussion traditions. As a matter of fact he belongs to a new generation of percussionists that feel at home in many different musical situations.

     Several projects allowed him to develop his skills in a wide range of styles. Performances and Workshops have taken him across Europe and Southamerica. Recently he shared the stage with artists such as Grossi, Lilian Vieira, Rogerio Souza, Izaías de Almeida, Papo de Anjo, Jorge Pardo, HKU Bigband, Royal Conservarory Big Band. Over the past few years Roman has performed on internationally recognized stages such as the North Sea Jazz Festival, Maison du Bresil, Tivoli Vredenburg and the Sparkassenarena Kiel.

     During his studies Roman attended masterclasses with Marsalis, Marcos Suzano, Sergio Krakowski, Nils Fischer, Changuito and Egberto Gismonti. In 2015 he obtained his Bachelor of Music at the Rotterdam Conservatory where he studied Latin and Brazilian Percussion at Worldmusic Department. As well as his principal study focus, of percussion and music theory/solfege, Roman also studied pedagogy.

     After his graduation he received a scholarship by the German Academic Exchange Organization to focus on his research of brazilian music.
